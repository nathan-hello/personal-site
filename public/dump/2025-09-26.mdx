# dump

Every year, for the past five years or so, I've been bouncing between different
reactions to the chaos of my media environment. I went from being completely
engaged, following the LSF drama posts, Destiny's chatroom, and watching the
unsexy side of CSPAN. Politics is what I was paying attention to, but very
little of it was political. I eventually saw that I was treating all of it as
entertainment, and recoiled back heavily. This was my "grill-pill" era. When a
story blows up on twitter, especially if the posts are the story instead of a
reaction thereof, and you meet a cast of characters, just to never see them
again, wasn't that all a waste? Seeing these stories play out in real time is
the treat, and that's why people follow sports, LSF, and the news.

When my friends would have some petty disagreement, I would be the one in the
group to point my finger up in the air and say "Uh, guys, you know this
literally doesn't matter right?". We were talking about branding one day, as I
mentioned that I go out of my way to avoid wearing company logos, and my friend
said "You know avoiding logos is just as cringe as the Supreme people right?".
His point was that the fact that it affects me emotionally means that I'm
giving an amount of attention that something like a logo doesn't deserve. I
still disagree with this point, though I've softened a bit. I think that it's
weird that people are out here paying money to be billboards to brands that I
know for a fact have no personal value to the individual. I have attachments to
brands, sometimes they mean something significant to me. I may find myself
wearing some Framework merch because I believe in the company and their vision. 

"Grill-pill" was just softcore nihilism in disguise. I just wasn't interested
in hearing about the day to day things anymore. I started to understand why
people watch junk TV because if CSPAN was no different, then I also fell into
the maximizal media consumption trap. 

As always, now I feel like I've struck a half decent balance. I'll read the occassional
article, but I mostly keep up with the news with clips from tiktok. There are some
real commentators, and fewer but still some journalists on there now, so I know I'm
not getting the full picture but that's fine. If I'm curious about something, I know
where the primary sources are at, or short of that commentators that I trust. Now
my problem is the passive media consumption, not the intense sports watching but the
hundred videos that I forget ten minutes after I watch them. The problem is that every
so often I find someone new on Tiktok that completely changes my perspective on something
or gives an emotion that I've never been able to articulate some structure that I resonate
with that I think about it all day.
